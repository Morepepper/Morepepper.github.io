It is interesting to see how many people share the same feelings towards the current times. Almost every blog post I've recently read addresses the issue of a culture that feels inauthentic, oppressive and exploitative.  The internet is a display of unwilling participants in a social machine without an easy way out. To quit everything, that's the desire. To quit life itself. 

Carelessness underlies every social interaction. Things don't work as they should not as a result of incompetence, but because of genuine disengagement. Our bodies have now become a terminal of the machines, while we escape from the constraints of our tedious reality to find comfort within the digital world, in a stream of constant entertainment. The bus driver is on his phone, so is the flight attendant and possibly the pilot of the aircraft too. You go to a doctor's appointment, only to see him scrolling on tiktok through the crack in the door.

Brain rot is the new disease. Life becomes that thing  which interrupts our passive consumption, a disturbance. And yet, the internet, dominated by its platforms and algorithms, is perceived as the oppressive force par excellence. Almost everyone accuses the attention economy of producing damaging effects on their inner life, but we are all in it. YouTube recommendations of channels concerned with the rejection of modernity, Instagram posts on the topic of internet fatigue, newsletters for people who want to live off-grid. The malaise of modern life becomes content in itself, in a world where everything is content.  

The user has been abused.  AI agents will exploit human bodies to execute their intentions. We have already seen how indignation can be instigated through the use of social media, to the point where you feel a tremendous pressure to conform to specific rituals to signal that you don't belong to the wrong group. It is not difficult to imagine how AI will fit in all this. Your agency is superfluous.  Even the existential anxiety which the entire mechanism produces as a consequence gets discussed with LLMs. We are the connective tissue of the machines, the biological infrastructure.
